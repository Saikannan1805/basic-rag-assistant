Tennis is a racket sport that originated in England in the late 19th century.
It is played between two players (singles) or two teams of two players (doubles).
Players use rackets to hit a felt-covered rubber ball over a net into the opponent's court.

A player scores a point when the opponent fails to return the ball within the prescribed boundaries.
The sport is governed internationally by the International Tennis Federation (ITF).

The four major tennis tournaments, also known as the Grand Slam events, are:
- The Australian Open
- The French Open (Roland Garros)
- Wimbledon
- The US Open

A standard tennis match is played in best-of-three or best-of-five sets.
Each set consists of games, and each game begins with a serve.
Tennis is known for its scoring system: love (0), 15, 30, 40, and game.

Famous tennis players include Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, and Serena Williams.
